Insights into Australia's Education Export Activity

Australia's Education exports are an important component of the country's economy, accounting for 40% of all Australia's service exports. Substantial growth in Education exports has coincided with the rise in wealth among Asian countries, particularly China and India, where accelerating GDP and income growth has encouraged demand for Australia's education services.

Trend in Education Exports for Australian States

New South Wales and Victoria have been the largest benefactors of the growth in Education exports, both accounting for the majority of exports in Australia. Total Education exports in Australia was valued at $40,337 million as at 2019. There are a few reasons for NSW, VIC and QLD holding the majority of export revenues. For one, 6 Universities ranked top 60 in the world are located in these States (QS Rankings). Melbourne (VIC) and Sydney (NSW) are also regarded as some of the world's most liveable cities.

Trend in Sources of Demand for Education Exports

China is the largest source of demand for Australian Education services. Although India is the next largest contributor to Education exports, export value is significantly lower than China by $6,252 million, as at 2019. Australia generates the majority of its Education exports from Asian countries, as highlighted by the top 5 export countries. According to the Reserve Bank of Australia, this is attributed to Asia's growing GDP and Income levels resulting in higher demand, Australia's close proximity, and English course offerings which are particularly attractive to overseas students.

Global Proportion of Education Export Demand

The choropleth map highlights countries that contribute a relatively greater proportion of Australia's Education exports with a darker colour, and countries that contribute a relatively smaller proportion of exports with a lighter colour. Note that the greatest contributors are primarily within Asia, and countries that virtually do not spend on Education services are coloured in grey.

Onshore and Offshore International Students at all Australian Universities

Universities in the state of Victoria cater for more offshore international students than any other state. However, the University of Sydney caters for the most onshore international students out of all universities. This is because these Universities have international campuses and are actively involved in overseas programs for students. Both New South Wales and Victoria cater for the majority of international students. One reason may be the prestige and global rankings of Universities in NSW and VIC, which are particularly attractiv to overseas students; QS ranked The University of Melbourne and Monash University 38 and 58, respectively, in 2020, whereas The University of Sydney and the University of New South Wales were globally ranked 42 and 43, respectively.

Popularity of Courses among Overseas Students

The most popular courses for international students are Management and Commerce, Information Technology, and Engineering and Related Technologies. Popularity is influenced by future career prospects, such as the emphasis on technology in many fields, as well as the benefits towards permanent residency chances if an in-demand qualification is pursued, such as Accounting. Furthermore, overseas students will gravitate towards certain courses that top universities are renowned for, such as University of Melbourne's undergraduate Commerce degree.
